## 2. What Went Well

1. **`Result<T, Failure>` + sealed `AppError` (Story 1.3) is paying compound interest.** Every later story pattern-matched on it without inventing new error contracts. Only one conscious exemption (Story 1.6 `CapturePreferenceStore` fallback-to-default) — and it was caught at review and flagged as a decision, not drift.
2. **Abstract-interface seam pattern** (`SecurityService` / `FakeSecurityService` from Story 1.3) propagated cleanly into `ConsentService` (1.4) and `PermissionService` (1.6). When 1.6's `CapturePreferenceStore` skipped the seam, reviewers caught it immediately. Pattern is now canon.
3. **`EvisitorFakeAdapter` test transport** (built in 1.3, scripted in 1.7, reused in 1.8 cookie-jar persistence) saved an entire mock-server infrastructure. No docker-compose, no external dependencies.
4. **Story 1.7 documented six concrete eVisitor response shapes** (success-with-cookies, 200+envelope, 200+`false`, 200+lockout, 200+API-key-break, 400+UserMessage). This is the test matrix for Epic 2 Story 2.2 — pre-written.
5. **`// TODO(story-2.x):` migration markers** convention (formalized in Story 1.7's "Epic 2 consolidation contract" section) gives Epic 2 explicit hand-off contracts at every seam: dioProvider, classifier, LoginNotifier→AuthNotifier, lockout persistence, error mapping.
6. **`@Riverpod(keepAlive: true)` for process-lifetime providers** consistently applied (consent, router, security, credential store, dio, cookie jar). Cohesive convention.
7. **Reviewer discipline on tautological tests was excellent.** Caught "would still pass after deleting the code under test" in 1.1 (`MainApp` smoke), 1.2 (token grid), 1.3 (CertPins positive path), 1.7 (cookie-jar assertions). This is the single most valuable habit to keep into Epic 2.
8. **CI hygiene grew with the codebase.** `dart format` scope rewrite (1.1), free-disk-space action (1.2), macOS-runner golden-tests job (1.7). Each story's CI lessons survived into the next.

### 3.2 Story sizing miss

**Story 1.7 was ~2× any other story.** 938 lines, 13 tasks, 170 tests, +51 new. Combined login screen, error classifier interim, lockout timer, FLAG_SECURE wiring, MethodChannel native code, cookie-jar persistence verification. Should have been split into **1.7a** (login + classifier) and **1.7b** (lockout + FLAG_SECURE + cookie verification). Lesson: when a story has six independent verbs in its title, split.

### 3.3 Process miss in Story 1.9

**Story 1.9 shipped as one squashed commit instead of per-task commits.** The commit-per-task rule held for 1.1–1.8 but cracked under what looked like a "small follow-up" story. Pushed already; can't rewrite history. Reinforce in Epic 2 — the rule is not size-conditional.

### 3.4 Dependency-reality friction (every story hit ~1)

- Story 1.2: Manrope GitHub 404; CI disk space mid-flight.
- Story 1.3: Riverpod 3.x incompatible with Dart 3.10.7 → pinned at 2.6.x. **This ceiling cascades through every later story.**
- Story 1.4: `google_mobile_ads` shipped 8.0.0 (vs spec 6.x); sync APIs became `Future<T>`.
- Story 1.5: `go_router` 17.x demanded Riverpod 3.x → pinned at 14.8.1 (the Riverpod 3 ceiling propagating).
- Story 1.7: Croatian ICU plural `=3`/`=4` rejected by gen-l10n (CLDR `few` category); FLAG_SECURE MethodChannel design.
- Story 1.8: `pumpAndSettle` hung on FlutterSecureStorage platform channel.

**Budget for it: expect ~1 dependency surprise per story in Epic 2. Don't let it derail estimates.**

## 6. Significant Discoveries — Epic 2 Plan Likely Needs Updating

These are not blockers, but they are real holes in Epic 2's current AC set. **Recommend a 30-minute Epic 2 plan-refinement pass before Story 2.1 starts.**

1. **Architecture doc stale path.** Line 673 still references `lib/features/onboarding/consent_screen.dart`. Trivial fix.
2. **`SessionBootstrap` (1.8) → `AuthState` (2.1) is not 1:1.** Story 2.1 lists `Initial | Unauthenticated | Authenticating | Authenticated | Reauth | LockedOut | AuthFailure`. Story 1.8 ships `SessionBootstrap` with 4 variants and asserts a 1:1 mapping — but `BootCookiesMissing` has no obvious AuthState peer, and `BootCredentialsMissing` collapses into `AuthFailure(credentialsInvalid)`. **Story 2.1 AC must include the explicit transition table** or the BootGate handoff will leak undefined states.
3. **Lockout persistence is a new requirement, not a port.** Story 1.7's lockout timer is `autoDispose` — state is lost on back-gesture / process death. Story 2.5 currently reads as "circuit breaker, 3 failures, 6-min open." It must explicitly persist `lockedOutUntil: DateTime?` to encrypted storage, otherwise host force-quits the app and the lockout vanishes. **Add to Story 2.5 AC.**
4. **`LoginResponseClassifier` (1.7) takes `Response`, not `DioException`.** Story 2.2 says "classifier consumes `DioException`." Real classifier must handle both axes: 200-success-with-error-envelope **and** thrown `DioException`. **Story 2.2 needs a unified `ClassifierInput` ADT or two entry points.**
5. **`AuthInterceptor` + `Ref` reentrancy footgun.** See C3 above. Could break Story 2.3 if not spiked first.
6. **`CredentialBanner` via `MaterialBanner` subclass + `ShellRoute` overlay (Story 2.7) does not match current router.** Epic 1 uses a flat `routerProvider`. Story 2.7 will refactor router shape and ripple through 1.5/1.8/1.9 redirects. **Either scope ShellRoute introduction explicitly into 2.7 or split it out.**
7. **Riverpod 2.6 vs. 3.x decision is the gating item.** See C1.
8. **Per-task commit discipline cracked in 1.9.** Reinforcement is a process action (A1), not an epic-update issue.

**Recommended action:** before kicking off Story 2.1, do a focused refinement pass on Story 2.1 (item #2), Story 2.2 (item #4), Story 2.5 (item #3), Story 2.7 (item #6). Items #1 and #5 are work, not refinements.

## 8. Key Takeaways (for Epic 2 and beyond)

1. **The seam pattern works.** Abstract interface + concrete + fake. Don't break it for "small" stores.
2. **`Result<T, Failure>` is the spine.** Don't carve exemptions without recording them as decisions.
3. **Spec dry-run before kickoff.** Five mid-flight amendments in one epic is the symptom, not noise.
4. **Story 1.7 was a stress test — it survived because the foundation (1.3 Result/AppError, 1.3 EvisitorFakeAdapter) was solid.** That validates the JIT order: build the seam before the feature that needs it.
5. **`mounted` guards and re-entrancy guards belong in a checklist or a utility.** Not in five separate review cycles.
6. **Reviewer-caught tautological tests is the lowest-cost bug in the system — but it's still cost.** Self-catch saves a turn.
7. **Per-task commits is not a soft preference.** 1.9 broke it under "small follow-up" pretense — that's exactly where it matters most.
8. **Epic 2 inherits a clean handoff catalog** (`// TODO(story-2.x):` markers + Story 1.7's consolidation table). Use it.
