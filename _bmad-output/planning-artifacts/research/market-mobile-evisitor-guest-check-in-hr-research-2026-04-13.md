## Customer Behavior and Segments

*Sources: Croatian Bureau of Statistics (DZS) first-release tourism release; Croatian National Tourist Board (HTZ) press; gov.hr / industry explainers for eVisitor rules. Confidence: **high** for DZS commercial-accommodation figures; **medium** for behavioral/psychographic generalizations (academic/sharing-economy literature not Croatia-specific).*

### Customer Behavior Patterns

**Behavior drivers**

- **Compliance-first workflow:** Private hosts operate under **mandatory guest registration** in the national system (eVisitor). Industry explainers frame this as a recurring administrative task tied to **tourist tax / sojourn tax** collection and oversight, not an optional “nice to have” ([Chekin — Croatia eVisitor explainer](https://chekin.com/en/blog/croatia-evisitor-what-is-it-how-to-register-your-vacation-rental/); [Hostaway — Croatia Airbnb rules / eVisitor](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-rules-croatia/)).
- **Bursty, door-side work:** Tourism is **concentrated on the Adriatic** (DZS: **94.3%** of tourist nights in the Adriatic region in 2025). Private hosts in coastal counties face **peak-season check-in waves**—behaviorally similar to “batch at the door, clean up admin later,” which aligns with a **scan-first / send-later** product narrative (product hypothesis; **not** directly measured in DZS tables).
- **Platform-mediated discovery, national compliance:** Hosts often acquire guests via **OTAs** (Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.) but must still satisfy **Croatian** registration rules independent of the platform ([Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-rules-croatia/)).

**Interaction preferences**

- **Mobile-first operations** are plausible for hosts who move between units and meet guests on site; segment is **Android-first for your MVP** per product scope (behavioral fit: plausible; validate in interviews).
- **Trust and ID handling:** Travelers and hosts routinely negotiate **passport/ID data** for registration; public discussions exist in forums/social ([example discussion — r/croatia](https://www.reddit.com/r/croatia/comments/o920kc/should_we_share_our_id_number_and_other/))—relevant to UX copy and perceived safety, not to market size.

**Decision habits**

- **Risk avoidance:** Non-compliance is described by industry sources as exposing hosts to **fines, suspension, registry removal, retroactive tax** ([Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-rules-croatia/)). That favors tools that **reduce error** and **prove submission** (your 30-day local history maps to “receipt of record” psychology).
- **Cost sensitivity:** Private hosts range from **side-income** “spare room” operators to **semi-professional** multi-listing hosts; willingness to pay for a **single-purpose utility** is unlikely to mirror SaaS budgets of property managers (hypothesis—pricing step).

### Demographic Segmentation

**Geographic distribution (demand side for your product)**

- DZS **commercial accommodation, 2025:** **20.7 million** tourist arrivals and **94.8 million** tourist nights; **89.4 million** nights in the **Adriatic region** (**94.3%** of total nights) ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)).
- **Holiday and other short-stay accommodation** (NKD group aligned with apartments / short-stay): **46.7 million** nights (**49.2%** of all tourist nights in commercial accommodation), **269 thousand** available units (**61.0%** of all units), **718 thousand** permanent beds (**61.9%** of beds) ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)).

**Private-host “segment” (supply side)**

- Official stats describe **accommodation groups and beds**, not “number of natural-person Airbnb hosts.” **Headline alternative:** Croatia Week reported on the order of **~125,000 registered private accommodation facilities** in a 2024/2025 policy context ([Croatia Week — accommodation landscape](https://www.croatiaweek.com/croatias-accommodation-landscape-set-for-big-change/))—use as **order-of-magnitude only**; cross-check with ministry/registry sources when you need a hard GTM TAM for “hosts.”
- **Capacity contraction:** DZS notes **fewer** accommodation units and beds in 2025 vs 2024 (units **−1.4%**, beds **−1.6%**), with **holiday/short-stay** units down **5,000** (−1.9%)—regulatory/capacity pressure is real at the macro level ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)).

**Age (tourists, proxy for family vs party travel)**

- DZS: tourists **up to 14** realised the **largest share** of nights (**18.5%**), then **35–44** (**17.4%**) ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)). Implies **family travel** is material; check-in batches may include **multiple documents per arrival** (product implication for throughput).

### Psychographic Profiles

_General sharing-economy literature_ segments hosts (e.g. **economically motivated “professional”** vs **socially motivated occasional** hosts). That literature is **not Croatia-specific**; apply as **weak prior** only.

- **Values:** **Income and asset utilization** vs **hospitality/identity** as hosting motives (see e.g. discussion of host heterogeneity in platform research—[SAGE / Airbnb crisis paper](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/27652157251385664) as example of segmentation framing).
- **Attitudes:** Hosts who perceive **enforcement risk** as salient will favor **predictable, audit-friendly** workflows (maps to your **block submit on bad fields**, **duplicate scan warning**, **human-readable errors**).

### Customer Segment Profiles (ICP-aligned)

**Segment A — Coastal private host, 1–3 units, peak-season heavy**

- **Demographics/geography:** Adriatic counties; DZS shows Istria, Split-Dalmatia, Primorje-Gorski Kotar among top counties by volume ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)).
- **Behavior:** High **check-in throughput** in July/August; strong fit for **session-based scanning**, **queue**, **batch send**.
- **Tech:** Smartphone-centric; **Android-first** acceptable if share matches regional device mix (validate locally).

**Segment B — “Spare apartment” host, shoulder season**

- **Behavior:** Lower frequency; **pain** is **forgetting steps** / **procrastination**, not throughput. Your **deferred login until first send** reduces friction for infrequent use (product fit).

**Segment C — Multi-facility on one OIB (your roadmap)**

- **Behavior:** Switching context between objects **without** retyping credentials; DZS capacity stats show **many small units** exist in holiday/short-stay (**269k units**), supporting **multi-object** pain as plausible—**fraction** actually operated by one legal person requires **registry or survey** (gap).

### Behavior Drivers and Influences

| Driver | Mechanism | Implication for product |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Legal obligation** | Guest registration in eVisitor; fines/suspension stressed by industry guides ([Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-rules-croatia/)) | Position as **compliance throughput**, not “cool OCR” |
| **Seasonality** | Massive concentration of nights in **holiday/short-stay** and on the coast ([DZS](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)) | Peak **UX** (speed, glare, outdoor use) matters more than mid-winter |
| **Macro regulation** | EU/Croatia reporting on **stricter short-term rental** rules ([EU Tourism Platform summary](https://transition-pathways.europa.eu/tourism/news/croatia-introduces-tougher-tourism-and-property-rules-curb-coastal-apartment-inflation)) | Long-term **supply** may shrink or professionalize—pricing and churn risk |

### Customer Interaction Patterns

**Research and discovery (for a utility app)**

- Hosts likely discover tools via **Play Store** search (“eVisitor,” “turisti,” “prijava”), **Facebook groups**, **host forums**, and **word of mouth** in coastal towns—**not** identical to B2B SaaS inbound (channel hypothesis for GTM).

**“Purchase” decision**

- Low-friction trial: **free tier** or **low single-digit €/mo** vs **time saved** at check-in; **substitute** is **manual web eVisitor** + stress.

**Post-purchase / retention**

- **Reliability** (successful send, understandable errors) and **speed** beat feature breadth for a **narrow utility**—aligns with your **v1 scope fence**.

## Customer Pain Points and Needs

*Web-verified where cited. **Competitor app review text** was not bulk-extracted in this pass—listings exist ([PrijaviTuriste](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=hr.prijavituriste), [mVisitor](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=uno.intersoft.mvisitor)); for differentiation, run a **1–2 star / recent negative** scrape or AppFollow-style export before claiming specific bugs.*

### Customer Challenges and Frustrations

**Primary frustrations (system + process)**

- **Mandatory fields and volume:** Each guest requires a **long set of fields** (identity, stay window, tourist-tax category, etc.). Official FAQ enumerates extensive **required** fields for check-in/out ([eVisitor FAQ — “Prijava i odjava turista” / mandatory fields](http://www.evisitor.hr/eVisitorWiki/Javno.eVisitor-Cesto-postavljana-pitanja.ashx)).
- **Time pressure at scale:** Industry content describes the **Friday check-in → “I’ll register tomorrow”** failure mode and **stacking unregistered guests** when throughput exceeds attention ([Rentlio guide](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)).
- **Deregistration as a second failure mode:** Same source stresses **forgotten checkout** as common—registration without deregistration breaks tax/guest-state correctness ([Rentlio guide](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)).
- **Manual entry error risk:** Foreign names, dates, and document numbers are called out as **typo-prone** when typed by hand ([Rentlio guide](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)).
- **Identity / OIB validation friction:** Official FAQ documents **strict matching** between OIB and name variants and paths to resolve mismatches via support ([eVisitor FAQ — physical person entry](http://www.evisitor.hr/eVisitorWiki/Javno.eVisitor-Cesto-postavljana-pitanja.ashx))—hosts hit **opaque validation errors** not “business logic” errors.
- **Corrections require paperwork:** FAQ states that **data changes** that affect financial obligations may require **re-attaching supporting documents**—high friction for “small fixes” ([eVisitor FAQ — object / correction rules](http://www.evisitor.hr/eVisitorWiki/Javno.eVisitor-Cesto-postavljana-pitanja.ashx)).
- **Operational workaround when system is down:** Rentlio’s FAQ explicitly contemplates **eVisitor downtime at check-in** and **buffering data for later submit** within 24h ([Rentlio guide FAQ](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide))—confirms **real-world availability pain** (your **queue + manual send** aligns).

**Usage barriers**

- **Credential / admin complexity:** FAQ references **TAN lists**, **primary vs sub-users**, director changes—onboarding and account hygiene are non-trivial ([eVisitor FAQ — contact / user admin](http://www.evisitor.hr/eVisitorWiki/Javno.eVisitor-Cesto-postavljana-pitanja.ashx)).
- **Bypass instead of compliance:** Press reporting describes **private renters skipping eVisitor** (especially when not on-site), i.e. some address pain by **avoiding the system** rather than better tooling ([Total Croatia News, 2023](https://www.total-croatia-news.com/news/croatian-private-renters-2/))—segments the market into **compliant tool buyers** vs **grey-zone avoiders** (latter not your honest ICP).

**Service / support pain points**

- **Tourism board / email loop:** FAQ pushes many edge cases to **tourist board** or **eVisitor contact email** rather than self-serve fixes ([eVisitor FAQ](http://www.evisitor.hr/eVisitorWiki/Javno.eVisitor-Cesto-postavljana-pitanja.ashx))—latency when blocked.

**Frequency**

- **Peak season:** Rentlio frames manual registration as manageable at low cadence, **breaking** at multi-property / high arrival rates ([Rentlio guide](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)); aligns with **July–August burst** behavior from Step 2.

### Unmet Customer Needs

| Unmet need | Why it’s “unmet” in baseline workflow | Your product hook (hypothesis) |
|------------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Fast capture → structured data** | Manual typing from ID | MRZ-first + edit-on-fail |
| **Defer submission without breaking law** | Need register within deadline; downtime / bad network | Local **queue**, send when online (still host responsibility to meet 24h—position as **risk reducer**, not legal bypass) |
| **Multi-object without context bugs** | Wrong facility / wrong unit under stress | **Session-scoped facility** (your SCAMPER “neutral app”) |
| **Proof of what was sent** | Host fear of fines / disputes | **30-day history** as receipt |
| **Understandable failures** | Opaque server/validation errors | **Croatian human errors** mapping |

**Critical unmet needs (evidence-backed):** **throughput + error reduction** (Rentlio); **correction friction** (official FAQ); **downtime buffering** (Rentlio FAQ).

**Market gaps:** **Standalone lightweight mobile** for host-only MRZ pipeline vs **full PMS** (Rentlio, Chekin-class integrations)—price-sensitive private hosts may not want **channel manager** bundles.

### Customer Satisfaction Gaps

- **Expectation vs reality:** Hosts may want **“scan and forget”**; reality is **validation**, **corrections**, **checkout**, **fines** if sloppy ([Rentlio fines range **€132–€2,654** cited from Croatian reporting in article](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)).
- **Value gap:** Integrated suites promise **end-to-end automation**; your wedge is **speed + clarity** for hosts who won’t buy PMS—must prove **minutes saved per check-in**.

### Emotional Impact Assessment

- **Shame / fatigue** from admin backlog (“three unregistered guests”).
- **Reputation risk** with guests when asking for ID (forum / travel threads).

## Customer Decision Processes and Journey

*Interpretation for **Croatian private hosts** choosing a **narrow mobile utility** (eVisitor capture/submit): industry buyer guides for **vacation rental / PMS** software are used as **analogous** decision frameworks (fit, pain, red flags, pilot)—not claims about your market’s measured behavior. ### Customer Decision-Making Processes

| Stage | What happens for this product class | Notes |
|-------|--------------------------------------|--------|

**Timelines**

- **Seasonal:** strongest intent **pre-season** (Apr–Jun) and **first bad peak weekend** (panic buy)—hypothesis consistent with tourism seasonality (not directly measured for this niche).
- **Evaluation** for low-ticket utilities is often **minutes to hours** (mobile “micro-moments”) per Google’s mobile shopper framework ([Think with Google — PDF on mobile decision journey](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/_qs/documents/37/mobile-redefined-consumer-decision-shopper-journey-b.pdf)).

**Complexity**

- **Low cognitive load** desired (host is at door); **high consequence** if wrong (fines)—so **trust + clarity** dominate over feature count.

**Evaluation methods (from adjacent STR software buying)**

- Identify **top operational pain points**, match software to **business model**, **scenario-based** demos rather than generic tours, **pilot** on subset before full commitment ([Key Data — vacation rental software selection](https://www.keydatadashboard.com/blog/vacation-rental-software)).
- Red flags: **poor integrations**, **vague timelines**, **inability to demo exact workflows**, **frequent negative reviews** ([Key Data](https://www.keydatadashboard.com/blog/vacation-rental-software))—translate to your context as: **unstable send**, **unclear onboarding**, **bad Play Store sentiment**.

### Strengths and Weaknesses (summary)

**Official eVisitor**

- **Strengths:** €0, authoritative, maintained by HTZ; full legal path.
- **Weaknesses (vs power users):** manual throughput, UX not optimized for **burst check-in** or **your** workflow hypotheses (from pain section).

**mVisitor**

- **Strengths:** **Free** via many TZs; **guest self-serve** (link/QR) reduces host typing; established training/docs ([TZ Brela](https://brela.hr/en/for-vacation-rental-owners/instructions-for-using-the-mvisitor-app)).
- **Weaknesses vs your v1 vision:** **guest-involved flows** conflict with **host-only** product boundary; less differentiation if you also ship guest links.

**PrijaviTuriste**

- **Strengths:** **Clear monetization** (pack pricing on site); **multi-account**; **gallery import**; **invoicing**; strong **social proof** on site; users cite **non-expiring** purchased registrations vs “other apps” (testimonials on [same site](https://evisitor-checkin.web.app/)).
- **Weaknesses / attack surface:** testimonials allege **eVisitor portal instability** (“sustav se često i rušio”)—**reliability** is a battleground; **gallery** path conflicts with your **v1 live-capture-only** security stance; **pricing per registration** creates **metering anxiety**—subscription or bundled pricing could win a segment.

**PMS-integrated (Rentlio / Chekin / Cloudbeds)**

- **Strengths:** one stack for **calendar + guest + compliance**.
- **Weaknesses for your ICP:** **overkill + cost** for **single-unit private hosts** who won’t adopt full PMS.

### Market Differentiation (where you can win)

Aligned with your roadmap (**Android-first, host-only, MRZ-first, session + queue, multi-facility, batch send, deferred auth**):

1. **Throughput architecture** — **scan-first / send-later queue** + **session-scoped facility** as explicit **poka-yoke** against wrong-object submits (not “another OCR wrapper”).
2. **Discipline vs feature sprawl** — **no guest self-check-in in v1** = clearer **GDPR story** for hosts who fear guest links; **MRZ-only** vs **gallery** = smaller attack surface (trade-off: fewer doc types).
3. **Trust on failure** — **human-readable Croatian errors** + **30-day local proof** address **fear of fines** better than generic stacks.
4. **Pricing wedge** — PrijaviTuriste’s **per-registration** packs ([€4.99 / 30 … up to larger packs](https://evisitor-checkin.web.app/)) invite a **predictable** alternative (e.g. flat seasonal / low subscription) for high-volume hosts—**validate willingness to pay**.

**Hard to verify without product:** OCR **accuracy/speed** benchmarks vs PrijaviTuriste / mVisitor on **real passports**—this is the **empirical** differentiation battle.

### Executive Summary

1. **Demand context (quantified at macro level):** Croatia’s **commercial** accommodation saw **94.8M** tourist nights in **2025**; **holiday / other short-stay** alone accounted for **46.7M nights (49.2%)** and **269k** accommodation units—private/small-rental supply is structurally large ([DZS TUR-2025-1-2](https://podaci.dzs.hr/2025/en/97094)). This supports **many potential host users**, but **does not** count installs of helper apps.

2. **Job-to-be-done:** Hosts must satisfy **eVisitor registration/deregistration** under time pressure; dominant pain is **compliance throughput**—speed, **fewer errors**, **forgotten checkout**, **opaque system errors**, occasional **downtime** (see **Customer Pain Points** and [Rentlio guide](https://rentl.io/en/blog/industry-related/evisitor-croatia-vacation-rental-guest-registration-guide)).

3. **Competitive reality:** The **official eVisitor app** is the **free** baseline ([Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=hr.tz.evisitor)). **mVisitor** is institutionally distributed (**TZ-provided free access** in examples like [TZ Brela](https://brela.hr/en/for-vacation-rental-owners/instructions-for-using-the-mvisitor-app)) and supports **guest self-entry** via link/QR. **PrijaviTuriste** monetizes with **per-registration packs** (e.g. from **€4.99 / 30** upward on [evisitor-checkin.web.app](https://evisitor-checkin.web.app/)) plus broader features (gallery, invoicing, multi-account). **PMS suites** automate eVisitor for pros ([Chekin](https://chekin.com/en/blog/croatia-evisitor-what-is-it-how-to-register-your-vacation-rental/), [Cloudbeds EVisitor](https://myfrontdesk.cloudbeds.com/hc/en-us/articles/30948258620827-How-to-connect-Croatia-Police-Reports-EVisitor)).

4. **Strategic wedge (for your v1):** Win on **host-only throughput architecture**—**session-scoped facility**, **scan-first / send-later queue**, **MRZ-first discipline**, **batch send**, **human Croatian errors**, **local proof history**—not on **feature parity** with invoicing/guest links. **Prove** time/error wins vs incumbents with **benchmarks** and **review mining**.

5. **Pricing implication:** Metered check-ins ([PrijaviTuriste](https://evisitor-checkin.web.app/)) create **budget uncertainty** for high-volume hosts—test **predictable** pricing (seasonal flat, low subscription) against documented pack economics.

6. **Stakeholder constraint (2026-04-13):** Intended ship model is **free + in-app ads**; acceptable **revenue band** is **coffee → hobby money** (seasonal niche utility), not a primary income target—so competitive **pricing** pressure (€0 incumbents, metered packs) is partly addressed by **ad-supported** positioning rather than **paid** v1.
